# StudyForge Development Progress

## About This Project

I built StudyForge as an AI-powered learning assistant while exploring AI, software engineering, and education technology.

The idea started from my own experience with studying and tutoring. I noticed that students often don't struggle because they lack resources, but because they don't always get explanations that match their level.

A textbook, video, or classroom usually gives one explanation.

But different students understand things differently.

I wanted to explore whether AI could make learning more personalised.

---

# My Development Journey

## Starting Point

I began with a simple idea:

"Can I build an AI tutor that helps students understand concepts?"

The first version was focused on getting a working system where students could ask questions and receive explanations.

---

# Challenges I Faced

## Making AI Responses More Useful

One of the first problems I noticed was that AI could answer questions, but the quality of learning depended heavily on how the explanation was given.

A beginner and an advanced student might need completely different explanations.

To improve this, I added:

- Learning depth options
- Different learning modes
- Subject detection


Learning depths:

- Quick
- Balanced
- Deep Dive
- Expert


Modes:

- Normal
- Exam Prep
- Hint Mode
- Concept Builder

---

## Turning a Chatbot Into a Product

I realised that a useful learning tool needs more than just an AI response.

It needs:

- organised conversations
- saved history
- better user experience
- structured data

This led me to build:

- multiple chats
- chat storage
- database integration
