# My Journey Building StudyForge

StudyForge started from a simple idea: could AI help students learn in a more personalised way?

While studying and tutoring, I noticed that many students don't always struggle because they lack ability. Sometimes they struggle because they don't have someone who can explain a concept in a way that matches their current understanding.

A textbook gives one explanation.
A classroom has one pace.
But students learn differently.

I wanted to experiment with building something that could adapt.

The first version of StudyForge was simple:
a student asks a question, and AI explains it.

However, while building it, I realised that creating an AI tutor is much harder than just connecting an API.

I faced several challenges:

## Making explanations better

The AI could answer questions, but sometimes the answers were too advanced or too generic.

I added learning depth controls so students could choose how they wanted concepts explained.

## Designing for real users

At first, the system was focused mainly on getting the AI working.

Later, I realised a real learning platform needs:
- separate users
- private conversations
- saved progress
- personalisation

This changed the direction of the project.

## Understanding the technology

Building StudyForge pushed me to learn:
- Python
- APIs
- databases
- software design
- AI workflows

The project is still evolving, but each version is an attempt to move closer to the original goal:

Making learning support more accessible and personalised.
